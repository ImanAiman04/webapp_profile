UPM (Universiti Putra Malaysia) is a prominent public research university located in Malaysia, specifically in Serdang, Selangor. It is one of the top universities in the country, known for its emphasis on research, innovation, and academic excellence. Originally established as the School of Agriculture in 1931, it became a university in 1971, with the goal of advancing agricultural and technological studies.

Some key features in UPM:

  1. Academic Excellence: UPM offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various disciplines, including agriculture, engineering, business, social sciences, medicine, and environmental studies. It is particularly well-regarded for its programs related to agriculture, forestry, and biotechnology.
  2. Research and Innovation: UPM is known for its strong research culture, with numerous research centers dedicated to scientific, technological, and agricultural innovation. The university has produced significant research outcomes in areas like plant science, biotechnology, and sustainable agriculture.
